## 1. User Pain Patterns This Week

1.  **AI Lacks Persistent Memory/Context:** "What’s the point of using this if the app doesn’t remember anything. I can ask it for advice on a Tuesday and then on Wednesday go back and ask clarifying questions and it does not remember our conversation from Tuesday." (ChatGPT 1⭐ review). Users are frustrated when AI forgets past interactions, making long-term projects or follow-up questions impossible across sessions (Claude, Gemini 1⭐ reviews also confirm this).
    *   **Sources:** ChatGPT (1⭐ review), Claude (1⭐ review), Google Gemini (1⭐ review).
    *   **Why current solutions fail:** Current AI apps often treat each chat as a new session or have limited memory buffers, failing to build a persistent, user-specific knowledge base.

2.  **AI Over-Moderation/Bias:** "It kept messing up the prompt... it called me suicidal? And sent me a number for help??" (Claude 1⭐ review). Users are frequently blocked, lectured, or outright banned by overly aggressive "safety filters" for non-harmful or creative prompts.
    *   **Sources:** Claude (1⭐ reviews: "Ban bc under 18?", "moderation system flags normal conversations", "Programmed to be morally superior to you"), Google Gemini (1⭐ reviews: "flagged my message as 'major safety violation'").
    *   **Why current solutions fail:** AI models are often trained with strict ethical guidelines that lead to over-filtering, preventing users from engaging in creative or nuanced conversations without arbitrary interruptions.

3.  **Severe AI Usage Limits/Throttling:** "You reach usage limits faster than any other ai service. It’s a bit ridiculous. I can use chat or grok or perplexity all day and not hit usage limits. Claude is the WORST about hitting usage limits..." (Claude 1⭐ review). Users, even paying subscribers, hit message caps quickly, rendering the apps unusable.
    *   **Sources:** Claude (1⭐ reviews: "hit free tier messages after one prompt", "limiting my messages for four hours is extreme", "data limits errors").
    *   **Why current solutions fail:** Cloud-based AI services incur high operational costs, leading providers to implement strict usage limits that frustrate users who expect consistent access.
